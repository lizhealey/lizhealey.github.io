Teaching

I have been on the teaching team of multiple graduate and undergraduate courses during my training across topics in electrical engineering, machine learning, and translational methods in bioinformatics. I was previously a Teaching Development Fellow and earned a teaching certificate by completing the Graduate Teaching Development tracks at MIT.

HST.016: Artificial Intelligence in Health Care I
Teaching Assistant | Harvard-MIT HST | 2024

Course on fundamental concepts at the core of artificial intelligence, as applied to health care problems. Topics included deep learning for clinical risk stratification, bias and fairness in clinical machine learning, and transformer models.

AISC 610: Computationally-Enabled Medicine
Data Science Mentor | Harvard Medical School | 2024

Course on computational methods for medical problems, focusing on data analysis and machine learning applications in healthcare.

BMI 707: Precision Medicine II - Integrating Clinical and Genomic Data
Teaching Fellow | Harvard DBMI | 2022, 2023

Graduate-level course on biomedical data science leveraging genomic and phenotypic data for precision medicine..

BMI 703: Precision Medicine I - Genomic Medicine
Teaching Fellow | Harvard DBMI | 2022, 2023

Graduate-level course on biomedical data science leveraging genomic data for precision medicine.

BMI 707: Deep Learning for Biomedical Data
Teaching Fellow | Harvard DBMI | 2021

Course on deep learning for biomedical applications, including neural networks, CNNs, and RNNs for medical data analysis.

ES 152: Circuits, Devices, and Transduction
Course Assistant | Harvard SEAS | 2018

Course on circuit analysis, electronic devices, and sensor transduction principles for undergraduate electrical engineering students.

ES52: The Joy of Electronics
Teaching Fellow | Harvard SEAS | 2017, 2018

Introductory electronics course on digital and analog concepts and hands-on circuit building for undergraduate students. Laboratory-based learning fostered practical understanding of electronic principles.
